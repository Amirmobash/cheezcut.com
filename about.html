About the inventor

CheezCut is built around a practical observation from real kitchen handling.

This project presents the story behind the idea and the inventor, Amir Mobasheraghdam. The goal was not to make a complicated machine, but to improve one small step in a meaningful way.

The inventor

Amir Mobasheraghdam is presented as the inventor behind CheezCut. The project reflects a simple but valuable design instinct: notice a repeated inconvenience, then create a focused tool that solves it more elegantly.

The philosophy

Good product ideas are not always massive inventions. Sometimes they are practical improvements that remove friction from everyday work. CheezCut is positioned exactly in that space.

Brand narrative

A concept that feels simple because it is supposed to.

The strongest product ideas often look obvious in hindsight. That is part of the appeal of CheezCut. Its value does not come from visual complexity—it comes from clarity of purpose.

The concept is aimed at a specific moment: opening pizza cheese packaging without the awkwardness of manual tearing or the extra step of using a knife. That focus gives the idea its strength.

CheezCut is not trying to be everything. It is trying to make one repetitive task feel smarter, cleaner, and more intentional.
