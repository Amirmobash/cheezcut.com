About the brand

An inventor-led concept shaped around a very specific kitchen task.

CheezCut is introduced as a project by Amir Mobasheraghdam. It does not try to be a broad platform or a crowded product line. It is a single idea presented clearly: make the removal of Gouda’s red wax outer layer more practical, more controlled, and more refined.

The inventor’s point of view

The project begins with observation, not with technical complexity. When a repeated manual step feels slow, inconsistent, or mildly frustrating, there is room for better design. CheezCut reflects that mindset.

The brand position

CheezCut is presented as a focused innovation concept: not loud, not over-claimed, and not overloaded with features. The strength of the idea is its clarity of purpose.

Why the idea matters

Good design can improve a small repetitive action without making it complicated.

Many useful products are valuable precisely because they solve a narrow problem well. CheezCut belongs in that category. It is aimed at one point in handling Gouda: the moment when the outer red wax must be removed before use.

By giving that moment its own dedicated tool, the process can become more consistent, more professional-looking, and less dependent on improvisation.

Audience potential

Relevant to kitchens, food businesses, and future product partners.

Professional kitchens

For workflows where repeated prep steps benefit from consistency and speed.

Restaurants

For teams that value cleaner handling and more professional preparation routines.

Food businesses

For operations that work with Gouda and may appreciate specialized utility tools.

Manufacturing partners

For companies interested in concept discussions, refinement, and productization paths.

CheezCut is not presented as a finished mass-market product. It is presented as a well-defined concept with a clear use case, professional visual direction, and room for further development.
