Inventor-led concept · Gouda wax utility

Remove the Gouda wax rind with more control, less friction.

CheezCut is a dedicated utility concept for separating the red wax outer layer around Gouda cheese. It is not a slicer, not a cheese-cutting tool, and not a generic package opener. Its purpose is focused and simple: engage the wax layer, lift it cleanly, and separate it with more speed, consistency, and control.

01

What it solves

Removing the outer wax rind around Gouda by hand can be repetitive, uneven, and sometimes annoying. Some users may even turn to a knife simply for speed.

02

What it does

CheezCut is designed to reach the edge of the wax, lift it, and separate it from the cheese body in a guided, controlled motion.

03

What it is not

It is not a sandwich-cheese slicer, not a shredded-cheese accessory, not a plastic-wrapper opener, and not an industrial machine.

A small repeated friction point, treated like a real design problem.

The strength of the idea is not complexity. It is specificity. CheezCut targets one exact step in Gouda preparation: removing the red outer layer faster, cleaner, and with more repeatable results.

CheezCut is currently presented as a concept-stage project by Amir Mobasheraghdam. The site is designed for presentation, discussion, and early-stage interest. It is not currently an active sales site.
  • Better speed for a repetitive task
  • More visual neatness and process consistency
  • Lower reliance on hand force or improvised tools
  • A more professional feel in food-prep workflows

The idea in three steps

This concept is built around a simple functional sequence: engage the outer layer, lift it away from the cheese, and separate it with more control.

Engage

Reach the wax edge or rim and create a reliable starting point.

Lift

Raise the outer wax layer without turning the task into a messy hand operation.

Separate

Guide the layer away from the cheese body in a cleaner, more deliberate motion.

Who the concept may speak to

This kind of focused utility tool may be relevant to professional kitchens, food-prep businesses, cheese handling environments, kitchen-tool manufacturers, and early-stage product partners.
