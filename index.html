Inventor-led concept · Gouda-specific utility

A dedicated tool concept for removing the red wax rind around Gouda.

CheezCut is built for one exact task: separating the red outer wax layer around Gouda more cleanly, more deliberately, and with less dependence on fingers or knives. It is not a slicer, not a cutter for the cheese itself, and not a generic opener for ordinary plastic packaging.

01

The problem

The outer red layer around Gouda is often removed by hand. That process can be slow, uneven, repetitive, and in some situations frustrating enough that people reach for a knife.

02

The concept

CheezCut is a focused utility designed to engage the wax edge, lift it with control, and separate it from the cheese body in a cleaner motion.

03

The position

This concept is not about slicing cheese, serving portions, or opening ordinary wrapper bags. It is about one exact Gouda-prep interaction.

Why a small recurring friction point can deserve its own tool.

Strong product ideas are not always large systems. Sometimes they improve one annoying step that people repeat again and again.

CheezCut is currently presented as a concept-stage project by Amir Mobasheraghdam. The site is built for presentation, discussion, and early conversations with partners, manufacturers, and interested parties. It is not currently for sale.
  • Faster removal of the outer wax rind around Gouda
  • Cleaner and more repeatable handling
  • Reduced dependence on improvised knife use
  • A more professional feel for a specific food-prep task

The core motion: engage, lift, separate.

CheezCut is intentionally simple. Its job is to create a more reliable interaction with the rind rather than introduce a complicated workflow.

Engage

Reach the edge of the wax rind and establish a clean starting point.

Lift

Raise the outer layer without relying on a messy hand-only action.

Separate

Guide the rind away from the cheese body with more consistency and control.
